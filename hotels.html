HOTELS

Fairmont Banff Springs

Styled after a Scottish baronial castle, The Fairmont Banff Springs offers stunning vistas, championship golf courses, unparalleled skiing, several restaurants and a nice spa.

Best For: Families and couples who like all the amenities and facilities of a large resort.

Why: The location inside the national park and proximity to activities is unbeatable.

Where: Inside Banff National Park and near the town of Banff.

Number of Rooms: More than 750.

Fairmont Chateau Lake Louise

Similar to Banff Springs, Fairmont Chateau Lake Louise is a large resort with a myriad of amenities including several restaurants, a spa, golf and more.

Best For: Families and couples who like all the amenities and facilities of a large resort.

Why: The property has an incredible location inside Banff National Park and has spectacular vistas of Victoria Glacier and an emerald lake.

Where: Inside Banff National Park

Number of Rooms: More than 530.

Post Hotel & Spa

is a Relais & Chateaux property and one of the rare smaller hotels in the Rockies. It has an incredible spa, a destination worthy fine-dining restaurant and an expansive wine cellar.

Best For: Couples who enjoy fine dining and wines and like smaller hotels with personalized service.

Why: The incredible location, rich history and great service.

Where: In the village of Lake Louise in Banff National Park, close to Lake Louise

Number of Rooms: Close to a 100. Book rooms that face east and overlook trees and the hotel’s back lawns. West facing rooms overlook the parking lot and can be noisy.

Rimrock

is built into the side of a mountain in Banff National Park and has two restaurants and a small spa. It’s not as upscale as the Fairmont’s but offers fantastic views of the surrounding landscape.

Best For: Families and couples who enjoy the amenities of a large resort but don’t want to pay the high prices that the Fairmont’s command.

Why: Well-priced and well located.

Where: In Banff National Park, five minutes away from hot springs and Sulphur Mountain Gondola- a must-do attraction for every traveler.

Number of Rooms: More than 340

Moraine Lake Lodge

is on the shores of a glacial namesake lake and, with its timber food finishes, gives travelers an authentic sense of place. The lodge is only open in the summer and is the starting point for many of the most spectacular hikes in the Canadian Rockies. It’s widely regarded as the most upscale property in the Rockies and has high room prices to match. It doesn’t have many amenities, but with such a fantastic setting, service and aesthetic, guests won’t miss them.

Best For: Couples who like small properties and intimate service and have a big budget.

Why: The location and the feel of being in a true Canadian Rockies property.

Where: In Banff National Park about an hour from the town of Banff and 20 minutes from Lake Louise Village.

Number of Rooms: Around 30.

Fairmont Jasper Lake Lodge

is a 700-acre property in the heart of Jasper National Park. The property finished an extensive multi-year renovation in 2017 and has a fresher, more contemporary look. Amenities are many and include golf and a spa. Guests can also take advantage of lots of free on-site activities such as canoeing, kayaking, yoga by the lake and craft beer tasting.

Best For: Families and couples who enjoy the amenities of a large resort but prefer a serene setting.

Why: Spectacular location and less touristy and more secluded than the Fairmont’s in Banff National Park.

Where: In Jasper National Park on the shores of Jasper Lake.

Number of Rooms: More than 400

Emerald Lake Lodge

is a nature lovers retreat- the historic property, like all of Yoho National Park, has no cell service, and WiFi is only available in the main lodge. Amenities are limited, but there is a nice restaurant. Guest rooms are comfortable but somewhat spartan. However, they have balconies that provide stunning vistas of the surrounding mountains and wood burning fireplaces.

Best For: Families and couples who love seclusion and being in nature. They shouldn’t be hung up on staying at an ultra-luxe resort because this is not it.

Why: Spectacular off-the-grid location. A good value for money- nightly rates can be under a $100.

Where: In the heart of Yoho National Park.

Number of Rooms: More than 80.
