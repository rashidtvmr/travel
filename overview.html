CANADIAN ROCKIES

INFORMATION

Impossibly photogenic, the Canadian Rockies are a year-round destination and straddle the provinces of Alberta and Banff. They’re comprised of snow-capped peaks and alpine lakes and famous for their five national parks: Banff National Park is the biggest attraction, but there is also Jasper National Park, Yoho National Park, Kootenay National Park and Kananaskis Country National Park. The parks are adjacent to each other, and although most travelers stick to exploring Banff National Park, some visit multiple parks in a single trip.

THE BASICS

TIME DIFFERENCE FROM NEW YORK: Banff, Lake Louise and Jasper are in the Mountain Time Zone, two hours behind New York. Yoho and Kootenay, however, are part of British Columbia, and are in the Pacific Time Zone or three hours behind New York.

GETTING THERE: Calgary is a quick and easy 4.5 hour flight from New York and three hours from Los Angeles. Banff is a 1.5 hour drive from the airport while Jasper is three to four hours away.

THE WEATHER

The weather in the Canadian Rockies is variable: fall sees cool, crisp days while temperatures in the winter rarely dip below zero degrees Fahrenheit, keeping ski conditions favorable. Golfing and skiing are possible in the spring, and the summer is mild with temperatures rarely hitting above 80F. Summer is also when daylight hours can stretch until 11:00 p.m.

Temperature Chart

LAY OF THE LAND

The region’s five national parks are adjacent to each other. The resort town of Banff is situated in Banff National Park while the less touristy town of Jasper is in Jasper National Park. Driving is the best way to get around, but the journey between certain areas may take an hour or more. Lake Louise, for example, is an hour from Banff.

CURRENCY

The official currency in Canada is the Canadian dollar. The exchange rate between the U.S. dollar is almost equal. The latter is accepted in touristy destination, but in small towns and less visited spots, travelers should expect to pay in Canadian currency.

GOOD TO KNOW

Hotels in the summer, the peak time to visit the Rockies, get booked six months or more in advance.

PAIRS WELL WITH

The Rockies are a self-encompassing destination, but it is possible to add on a few days in Montreal or Toronto if clients are flying through either city.

THE FOOD AND ALCOHOL

The food in the Canadian Rockies is international with restaurants that will satisfy a range of taste buds. You’ll find Italian, Greek, Indian, sushi and Swiss fondue. Authentic Rocky Mountain cuisine includes an abundance of wild game such as venison, elk and bison, Alberta beef, river trout, locally foraged mushrooms and artisanal cheeses

Beer, Canadian and international wines as well as spirits are served at most restaurants.

INSIDER PLANNING TIPS

The Canadian Rockies are all about outdoor pursuits. Think hiking, skiing and scenic drives. Clients who enjoy culture may not appreciate the destination.

Advise clients to dress in layers in the summer as a single day can see temperatures ranging from 40 to 75 degrees. In winter, a heavy coat, winter boots, hat, gloves or mitts and warm clothing are essential. Be prepared to layer up or remove layers as temperatures change.

Drinking water can be problem in the park. Giardia or beaver fever is present in the streams and lakes, so using iodine tablets, water filters, or boiling to purify drinking water is recommended.

Clients shouldn’t attempt a hike without taking along storm clothing and proper footwear. Hiking boots and a good rain jacket are a necessity, even if the trail looks flat and the weather looks great -- conditions can change extremely quickly in the mountains. Clients should dress in layers. They could be hiking in 80 degree weather when they start and through ice just an hour later.

Clients MUST be apprised on bear safety tips through their guide. REMEMBER: They Are In Bear Country!

TIPPING

As in other parts of the world, tipping is purely discretionary. If you’ve gotten good service, it’s a good idea to tip.

Taxi drivers are not tipped, and the fare should be negotiated beforehand.

Restaurants: 15 to 20 percent of the bill

Guides: $20 per person per day

Local Drivers : $40 per person per day

Porters: $2 per bag
