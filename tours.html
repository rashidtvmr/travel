BANFF NATIONAL PARK AND LAKE LOUISE

Spanning 2,564 square miles of valleys, mountains, glaciers, forests, meadows and rivers, the UNESCO World Heritage Site of Banff National Park is one of the world's premier nature destination spots and a hiking wonderland. There are more than a 1,000 miles of trails here, and hikers can find anything from a one-hour jaunt up a mountain to a month long backcountry excursion into the lonely wild regions of the park.

The park is home to a number of outstanding geological and ecological features including hot springs and Canada’s longest cave system, Castleguard Cave. The park also contains Alberta's southernmost herd of the endangered woodland caribou.

TOP ACTIVITIES AND ATTRACTIONS

In summer, walk along the park’s many nature trails, hike into the backcountry or climb peaks made famous by earlier tourist-explorers. Other activities include golf, camping, glacier touring, boat tours, river rafting, horseback riding, cycling and fishing.

Drives

The Trans-Canada Highway, the Banff-Radium Highway, the scenic Bow Valley Parkway and the Icefields Parkway are all popular and extremely worthwhile drives, even for those who hate car trips. Bedsides the landscape, travelers may spot wildlife such as elk, deer, bighorn sheep, mountain goats, moose, black bears, grizzly bears, wolves and a host of other large and small mammals.

We highly recommended two drives: The Icefields Parkway, stretching between Lake Louise and Jasper, is a more than a 100 mile route of unsurpassed scenery. It is considered one of the greatest mountain drives in the world, and certainly the most spectacular in North America. The mountain peaks (often snowcapped) and many rivers, waterfalls, lakes and glaciers make this a must-do for every traveler. Remember, they can always book a driver and guide to navigate the roads. The highlight of the Parkway is Columbia Icefield, which is literally a large field of ice. Adventurous travelers can even walk across the ice. Bow Valley Parkway is a 30-mile route is a scenic route between Banff and Lake Louise. There are peaceful picnic areas, and beautiful short hikes along the way, with the Johnston Canyon hike as the most popular.

Lake Louise

A perfect composition of ice, rock, forest, water and cloud, Lake Louise is a natural masterpiece and offers an endless number of activities including incredible downhill skiing, photogenic hiking and walking and birdwatching.

We highly recommend two Lake Louise hikes: Plain of Six Glaciers Hike to Teahouse is probably the most famous and for good reason. Starting at the Chateau Lake Louise, the path follows the lakeshore and continues to the back of the valley where the views just get better and better. Glaciers, vaulting peaks, avalanche paths and high mountain wildlife are all on the menu. It’s eight miles round-trip. Lake Agnes to Teahouse is the second hike and a graded trail through old-growth forest that has expansive views of the mountains above Lake Louise. It’s four and a half miles round-trip. Moraine Lake: This emerald-green lake is set before a backdrop of sharp peaks and is ideal for canoeing and hiking.

YOHO NATIONAL PARK AND KOOTENAY NATIONAL PARK

YoHo National Park

Takaka Falls: This spectacular 1,250 foot waterfall is the second tallest in North America. In early summer, witness roaring run-off thunders as the spray glows with rainbows.

Natural Bridge: Get a view of Kicking Horse River, loaded with glacial silt, as it carves a path through ancient rock.

Emerald Lake: Great for canoeing, hiking, hiking, and horseback riding

Kootenay National Park

Pain pots: colorful mineral springs in bright red, green, purple and blue.

Marble Canyon: Sliced by Tokumm Creek, Marble Canyon shows off limestone gorges and a mesmerizing glacial blue creek.

Banff Town

The namesake town of Banff is the place to hit for shopping and restaurants. It has a diverse mix of locally owned stores ranging from chocolate boutiques to places selling fashionable activewear and plenty of eateries in all prices ranges to choose from.

Sulphur Mountain Gondola

Ride on this 8-person gondola 7,500 feet above sea level for a spectacular panorama of Bow Valley and its surrounding peaks. This ride offers the best view in the Canadian Rocky Mountains.

Hot springs

Relax by the Upper Hot Springs, a naturally warm-water pool that many claim has healing powers

Lake Minnewanka

is a great place to go for wildlife and bird watching, fly fishing, power boating and boat tours.

Johnston Canyon

A paved trail that clings to canyon walls and goes past beautiful waterfalls.

Hoodos

These spires are a natural wonder and were carved by the forces of wind and water.

Columbia Icefield

This massive field of ancient ice covers 125 square miles and reaches a depth of over 1,200 feet. It is composed of three glaciers: the Athabasca, the Dome, and the Stutfield Glacier.

GROUND PARTNERS

The Rocky Mountains have a dearth of high-quality DMCs. Embark works with the hotel concierges directly to hire private guides and plan activities for clients. The concierges at the Fairmont’s, in particular, are excellent.

In the winter, Snowy Owl Tours offers excellent and thrilling dog sledding tours.

POSSIBLE ACTIVITIES AND EXCURSIONS

  • Endless hiking opportunities for all fitness levels and ranging from one-hour to 10 hour treks.
  • Mountain biking.
  • Driving Icefields Parkway, considered one of the most scenic drives in the world.
  • Ice walking in winter.
  • Downhill, cross-country and heli-skiing.
  • Picnics.
  • Wildlife viewing safaris to see elk, bighorn sheep and grizzly bears.
  • Photo safaris.
  • Exploring the charming town of Banff.
  • Fly fishing on Lake Minnewanka.
  • Horseback Riding. The best horseback riding is by Lake Louise; otherwise, try a ride near Banff.
  • Float Tours. A float trip down one of the many rivers is perfect for relaxing and enjoying the wildlife.
  • White Water Rafting: Half-day rafting go down the Kananaskis River (from Golden in YoHo) and the Bow (from Canmore, near Banff).
  • Kayaking / Canoeing: One of the best ways to savor the beauty of these mountain lakes is to canoe or kayak on them. The most rewarding excursions are Moraine Lake and Vermillion lake (near Banff) with the backdrop of Mt. Rundle.
